Fake news and critical thinking in the digital era

What can you do as an individual to help tackle the problem of mis- and disinformation on the internet, both in terms of your personal and your professional life?

The biggest difference anyone can make to stop the spread of mis- and disinformation is on an individual level. There are a few key points on which everyone can improve on to become less susceptible to false information and “fake news”. These points that we will discuss works on both a personal and a professional level and should be strived for.


We believe that the biggest change an individual can make is to educate oneself. This means that we should take the time to understand the tricks and methods people use to try and manipulate us. As said in the article; “...familiarizing yourself with the tricks of the disinformation trade...” (Colleen, 2021), is an effective way of recognizing falsehoods before even starting to read.


However, another big reason why mis- and especially disinformation spreads so rapidly is the emotional aspects. “fake news” often uses this trick to great effect on many instances. We should be aware of our emotions before we read online, especially when reading something we are passionate about. “Research has found people are more susceptible to misinformation that aligns with their pre-existing views. This is called confirmation bias...” (Colleen, 2021), therefore we should be aware of any pre-existing personal biases we might have and try to approach it with a neutral standpoint without getting emotional.


One obvious point is that we should consider the sources. If there are no sources that directly connect to the content that is discussed, we should immediately become skeptical. We should consider whether the sources are trustworthy or not. We should also consider looking at multiple sources at the same time to confirm or refute if a source is reliable and trustworthy.
